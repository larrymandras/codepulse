<objective>
Assemble the lifecycle control surface: a self-contained `SkillLifecycleMenu` (⋯) that gates its items by the row's scope, blocks shadowed restores client-side (isShadowing + tooltip), guards multi-scope rows, owns the Move/Delete dialog state, calls `enqueueLifecycle`, and overlays the reused status badge for in-flight commands. Wire an always-visible ⋯ trigger into `SkillRow` (so it appears in every view that renders a row) and refresh `ColdStorageView`'s now-obsolete "/manage-skills in a terminal" copy.

Purpose: This is the final integration wave — the surface the operator actually touches. Making the menu self-contained (renders its own dialogs, calls the mutation directly) avoids threading callbacks through AllSkillsOverview/SkillsInCategory/ColdStorageView; SkillRow just renders `<SkillLifecycleMenu skill={skill} .../>` and the menu appears everywhere SkillRow is used.
Output: One new menu component + two focused row/view modifications, test-covered including the shadow-block and multi-scope guards.
